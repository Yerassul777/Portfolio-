# 1.  ABSTRACT

## 1.1 The research objective

Portfolio+ is a website that allows students from all over Kazakhstan to find opportunities - regional or international, online or offline, competitions, olympiads, or volunteer opportunities. There's something for every opportunity and every taste, allowing students to avoid browsing vague or outdated websites and find everything in a couple of clicks.

## 1.2 Hypothesis
The project hypothesis is that, by minimizing search complexity, students' engagement in both educational and extracurricular opportunities can be greatly increased through a centralized AI-based platform.
## 1.3 Research stages and procedures
The research stages included problem analysis, market research, platform designing, an MVP created based on HTML, CSS, TypeScript and AI engine (GPT chat) implementation, and usability test.
## 1.4 Methodology
The methodology involved user-centered design, analyzing current websites, data organization in the database, and the execution of a filtering algorithm depending on user questionnaires such as grade, city and so on. 
## 1.5 Novelty of the research
The unique feature of the project lies in combining opportunity aggregation, personalized recommendations based on AI, tools of portfolio tracking, and university matching based on your data.  
## 1.6 Results and conclusions
The results demonstrate the successful development of an effective MVP platform that will be able to filter on a personal level, assess with the help of AI, and structured portfolio planning.
## 1.7 Practical applications of the results.
The practical significance of the project is that it can enhance equal access to educational opportunities and help students to prepare for university applications.

# 2. INTRODUCTION
Portfolio+ is a digital resource aimed at helping students in the whole of Kazakhstan to find both education-related and extracurricular opportunities in their lives.
## 2.1 Relevance of the topic
Access to reliable information on academic competitions, olympiads, volunteer programs, and educational initiatives has been a major challenge to school students in Kazakhstan. Participating in such activities is essential in portfolio development, university admission, applying to scholarships, and generally academic development.

However, information about these opportunities is spread over various platforms such as individual school announcements, social media pages, university websites, and informal online communities. These materials tend to be out of date, partial or hard to follow. Moreover, the majority of the centralized platforms are oriented on international programs and do not pay enough attention to the regional and national opportunities in Kazakhstan.

As a result, many students are informed about major competitions and initiatives only at later stages of their academic journey. This is a slowed access to a structured and competitive portfolio. It is of particular concern to the students that represent smaller cities and rural regions, where the access to the updated information regarding education might be lacking.

With the world becoming digital and the current generation Z basing their information-seeking activities on the internet [1], the lack of centralized and organized opportunity discovery system leads to the inequality in access and constrained student educational capacity. 

Thus, it is relevant and needed to develop a single, digital ecosystem to pool the opportunities and offer a personalized direction.
## 2.2 Objective of the work
The main vision of this project is to develop educational and extracurricular opportunities for the students in Kazakhstan through a user-friendly web platform. It includes built-in additional features such as AI assistant, personal notes, and so on. This will allow portfolio development to be widely deployed across various cities in Kazakhstan.
## 2.3 Brief methods for solving the tasks were accomplished
To achieve this objective, we analyzed existing opportunity-discovery platforms and determined their limitations, created a minimum viable product (MVP), used a filtering algorithm using the characteristics of the users like grade level, city and interests, added an AI assistant that can offer customized advice and portfolio management and made it multilingual (Kazakh, Russian, English). 

Through solving these issues, the project will strive to help in better access to education, enhancing student academic competitiveness in the whole of Kazakhstan.

# 3. RESEARCH SECTION
## 3.1 Analytical review of known results
### 3.1.1 Opportunity discovery platforms
The rapid digitalization of education has resulted in the appearance of various platforms that offer access to academic materials, events, and extracurricular activities. Courses and professional networking are also available through international platforms LinkedIn and Coursera, but neither of them is orientated to aggregating opportunities and constructing portfolios at the school level. In Kazakhstan, opportunity information is typically distributed through individual school announcements, telegram and instagram pages and university websites.

However, these sources are not integrated and act separately. No centralized system combines the opportunities across regions and categories.

The studies conducted in educational technology have shown that central information systems enhance student engagement and reduce inequality in access to resources. According to reports by Tugba Dagli & Osman Cardak, digital personalization significantly improves user retention and participation in educational platforms [2].

### 3.1.2 Problem of information fragmentation evidences
The main limitations of existing solutions are fragmented information sources, lack of localization for Kazakhstan, absence of personalized filtering, no integrated portfolio tracking system and no AI-based advisory support. As a result, students spend excessive time searching for opportunities, often missing application deadlines or failing to discover relevant programs.

Recent studies revealed that Generation Z uses social media platforms widely in recent years as one of the main sources when searching information, including educational and personal development. A structural analysis of published reports has proven that Generation Z members use social media to search information in various fields of life, including health, education, and social life, which proves that digital platforms are relevant to the information-seeking of young people [3].

Additionally, feedback and interviews from professors from Nazarbayev University confirmed the growing importance of structured portfolios in university admissions.

This analysis confirmed the need to develop a single, AI-powered ecosystem that would be specifically unique to Kazakhstan.

## 3.2 Description of the methods for solving tasks
The project was implemented in several stages.
### 3.2.1 System architecture design
The application was created as web-based to make it accessible to all devices. Frontend technologies are HTML for structure, CSS as an interface styler, JavaScript and TypeScript for interactive functionalities.
### 3.2.2 Data organization
A database was designed to classify the opportunities based on type such as olympiad, volunteering, competitions and universities, format (online and offline), location (city and international), application deadlines, age and grade level groups.
### 3.2.3 Questionnaire-based algorithm of filtering
To cut down the complexity of search, a filtering algorithm was executed.
Input parameters such as city, age or grade level, suitable format and so on. The system applies conditional logic to present the relevant opportunities. This eliminates unproductive search results, and improves users efficiency.
### 3.2.4 AI integration
An assistant based on GPT was incorporated to offer personalized recommendations, portfolio performance evaluation, suggestions for skill improvement, and university pathway guidance.

## 3.3 Results and discussion
### 3.3.1 MVP development results
The minimum viable product includes a fully functional homepage, opportunity database interface, questionnaire-based filtering, AI assistant integration, portfolio notes section, university matching prototype.
### 3.3.2 Technical feasibility
Structured filtering logic has made the system run with minimal server load. The integration of AI can be considered a supportive human tool that can be used as an additional advisor but not to substitute human decisions to prevent any ethical or responsibilities misuse. The modular architecture allows future expansions such as mobile application development and automated deadline reminders. 
### 3.3.3 Innovation analysis
Portfolio+ is an innovation that is based on Kazakhstan localization, integration of AI with portfolio tracking, centralized academic opportunity system, university matching based on personal data. As compared to international portals, Portfolio+ focuses on a national education gap.

# 4. CONCLUSION
Access to educational and extracurricular activities to school students in Kazakhstan remains fragmented and inconsistent. The lack of a centralized information system makes it hard to give students a chance to browse and exploit the provided academic and volunteer programs. The Portfolio+ project solved this issue by creating a web-based platform that consolidates the opportunities by area and type and incorporates the personalization mechanism.
The most important outcomes of the project are recognition of information fragmentation as a barrier to student engagement, creation of a working minimum viable product, use of a filtering system of the questionnaire type that minimizes the complexity of search, incorporation of a GPT-powered assistant with a portfolio analysis and framework-specific recommendations, and portfolio tracking and university matching modules design.
Portfolio+ is an innovation, whose value-added feature is the localization of the product to Kazakhstan, the convergence of opportunities, AI personalization, tracking of portfolios, and university matching in the same ecosystem. The system eliminates the need to address educational gaps and regional accessibility issues directly unlike the global platforms. 
The upcoming growth can involve implementation of mobile apps, automatic deadline notifications, and the increased involvement of institutions. 
To sum up, Portfolio+ is an innovative solution acceptable on the scale and offers the modernization of the access to educational opportunities in Kazakhstan and helps to enhance the academic background of the younger generation.
